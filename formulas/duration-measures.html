Duration & Convexity

Interest rate sensitivity measures for fixed income securities

Macaulay Duration

$$D_{Mac} = \frac{\sum_{t=1}^{n} t \times \frac{CF_t}{(1+r)^t}}{P}$$

Variables:

  • $t$ = Time to cash flow (in years)
  • $CF_t$ = Cash flow at time $t$
  • $r$ = Yield per period
  • $P$ = Current bond price
Interpretation: Weighted average time to receive cash flows

Modified Duration

$$D_{Mod} = \frac{D_{Mac}}{1 + r/m}$$

Variables:

  • $D_{Mac}$ = Macaulay duration
  • $r$ = Annual yield
  • $m$ = Compounding frequency per year
Note: For semi-annual bonds: $m = 2$

Effective Duration

$$D_{eff} = \frac{PV_{-} - PV_{+}}{2 \times PV_0 \times \Delta r}$$

Variables:

  • $PV_{-}$ = Bond price when rate decreases by $\Delta r$
  • $PV_{+}$ = Bond price when rate increases by $\Delta r$
  • $PV_0$ = Initial bond price
  • $\Delta r$ = Change in interest rate (typically 1 basis point)
Usage: Best for bonds with embedded options

Duration-Based Price Change

$$\frac{\Delta P}{P} \approx -D_{Mod} \times \Delta y$$

Variables:

  • $\Delta P$ = Change in bond price
  • $P$ = Initial bond price
  • $D_{Mod}$ = Modified duration
  • $\Delta y$ = Change in yield
⚠️ Limitation: Linear approximation - less accurate for large yield changes

Convexity

$$C = \frac{1}{P} \times \frac{\partial^2 P}{\partial y^2}$$
Purpose: Measures the curvature of the price-yield relationship

Price Change with Convexity

$$\frac{\Delta P}{P} \approx -D_{Mod} \times \Delta y + \frac{1}{2} \times C \times (\Delta y)^2$$
Advantage: More accurate for large yield changes than duration alone

All formulas verified using Python calculations
