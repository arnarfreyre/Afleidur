Bond Pricing

Valuation formulas for fixed income securities

Bond Price with Semi-Annual Coupons

$$P = \sum_{t=1}^{n} \frac{C/2}{(1 + r/2)^t} + \frac{F}{(1 + r/2)^n}$$

Variables:

  • $P$ = Bond price
  • $C$ = Annual coupon payment
  • $r$ = Annual yield (with semi-annual compounding)
  • $n$ = Number of semi-annual periods
  • $F$ = Face value (par value)
⚠️ Important: For bonds with semi-annual coupons, always use semi-annual compounding!

Bond Price with Annual Coupons

$$P = \sum_{t=1}^{n} \frac{C}{(1 + r)^t} + \frac{F}{(1 + r)^n}$$

Variables:

  • $P$ = Bond price
  • $C$ = Annual coupon payment
  • $r$ = Annual yield
  • $n$ = Number of years to maturity
  • $F$ = Face value
Usage: Common for European government bonds

Bond Price with Varying Term Structure

$$P = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{CF_i}{(1 + r_i/2)^{2t_i}}$$

Variables:

  • $CF_i$ = Cash flow at period $i$
  • $r_i$ = Annual spot rate for period $i$
  • $t_i$ = Time in years to period $i$
Note: Each cash flow is discounted at its corresponding spot rate

Clean vs. Dirty Price

$$\text{Dirty Price} = \text{Clean Price} + \text{Accrued Interest}$$
$$\text{Accrued Interest} = C \times \frac{\text{Days since last coupon}}{\text{Days in coupon period}}$$

Definitions:

  • Clean Price = Quoted bond price (excludes accrued interest)
  • Dirty Price = Invoice price (includes accrued interest)
  • $C$ = Coupon payment for the period

Yield to Maturity (YTM)

$$P = \sum_{t=1}^{n} \frac{C}{(1 + YTM)^t} + \frac{F}{(1 + YTM)^n}$$

Definition:

YTM is the internal rate of return that equates the bond price to the present value of all future cash flows

⚠️ Note: No closed-form solution - must be solved iteratively (e.g., Newton-Raphson method)

Current Yield

$$\text{Current Yield} = \frac{C}{P}$$

Variables:

  • $C$ = Annual coupon payment
  • $P$ = Current bond price
Limitation: Ignores capital gains/losses and time value of money

Zero-Coupon Bond Price

$$P = \frac{F}{(1 + r)^n}$$

Variables:

  • $F$ = Face value at maturity
  • $r$ = Yield per period
  • $n$ = Number of periods to maturity
Application: Treasury STRIPS, zero-coupon corporate bonds
